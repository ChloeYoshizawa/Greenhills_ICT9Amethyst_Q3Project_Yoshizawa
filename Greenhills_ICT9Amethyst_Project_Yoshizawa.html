Fire is the state, process, or event of combustion in which a fuel or other material is ignited and combined with oxygen, releasing light, heat, and flame. a hot mass of matter, as in a fireplace or stove.
An unexpected fire is devastating, it can erupt in the blink of an eye and you can lose everything you own. In a fire, you can lose your personal and private property. Here I have listed 4 ways to prevent unexpected fire.



How to Prevent Fire Accidents and Keep Your Home Safe
Never leave food unattended and be sure to follow these tips when cooking: Stay in the kitchen while the food is being cooked. Establish a three-foot “child-free zone” around your stove. Keep the area around your stove clear of towels, paper, and anything else that can easily catch fire or burn.
To avoid the causes of fire, only flammable or combustible liquids should be placed in the safety cabinet. Other hazardous materials such as acids, bases, or corrosive liquids should be stored in acid and caustic cabinets or other types of non-combustible storage units.
Limit the number of devices you use and only plug them into the appropriate outlet. Avoid "octopus" connections, don't overload plugins. Disconnect devices when not in use. When replacing cables or other components, use good quality electrical products.
Do not leave lit pipes or cigarettes unattended. Always empty the ashtray carefully. Make sure your smoking materials are outside, cool and preferably damp before throwing them in the trash - never use a trash can. Keep matches and lighters out of the reach of children and buy child-resistant lighters.
